AP Computer Science Principles

Where: Offered at ACHS, Langston, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown

Overview: A survey course intended for students who have no other computing experience. Students who have already taken another computer programming course should not take this course.

Designation: AP, 1 quality point.

Prerequisites: Recommended Algebra I with a B or better.

Typical grade levels: 9–12

Pathway connections: AP CS A and Computer Programming Advanced.

AP Computer Science A

Where: Offered at ACHS, Langston, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown.

Overview: AP-level course in Java. Assumes that students are already comfortable in Python.

Designation: AP, 1 quality point.

Prerequisites: Strongly recommended the completion of Programming, Programming Advanced or AP Computer Science Principles with a grade of B or better or permission of the instructor.

Typical grade levels: 10–12

Pathway connections: DE Computer Programming II, Intensified.

Programming

Where: Offered at H-B Woodlawn, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown.

Overview: Introductory Python Programming Course.

Prerequisites: Successful completion of Algebra I.

Typical grade levels: 10-12

Pathway connections: Computer Programming Advanced, AP CS A.

Computer Programming Advanced

Where: Offered at H-B Woodlawn, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown.

Overview: Continues with concepts introduced in Programming, focusing on projects and object-oriented software concepts.

Prerequisites: Successful completion of Programming or AP CS Principles.

Typical grade levels: 10–12

Pathway connections: AP CS A

Introduction to Information Technology

Where: Offered at ACHS, Langston, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown.

Overview: Recommended (but not required) as an entry point for any students interested in computing. Focuses on understanding how computers and the internet work. Introduces some web design and programming concepts.

Prerequisites: None.

Typical grade levels: 9–12

Pathway connections: Programming

DE Computer Programming I

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Introduces problem solving and implementation of solutions using a high-level programming language in a structured programming environment. Includes concepts and practice of structured programming, problem-solving, top-down design of algorithms, a high-level programming language syntax, control structures, arrays, and an introduction into object-oriented programming.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA courses are CSC 221 (Fall) and ITP 225 (Spring). Aligns with CS Associates.

Prerequisites: DE Web Page Design and Multimedia, Algebra II, coreq with DE Pre-Calculus.

Pathway connections: DE Computer Programming II, Intensified.

DE Computer Science II

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Introduces the concepts and techniques of object-oriented programming to students with a background in procedural programming and problem solving. Uses a high-level computer language to illustrate and implement the topics.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA courses are CSC 222 (Fall) and CSC 208 (Spring). Aligns with CS Associates.

Prerequisites: DE Computer Programming I, DE Pre-Calculus, coreq DE Calculus with analytic geometry.

Pathway connection: DE Computer Programming III, Advanced.

DE Computer Science III

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Examines the hierarchical structure of computer systems. Explores the representation of instructions and data, memory organization and structure, structure of a CPU, programming hierarchy and operating system interactions.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA courses include CSC 215 (Fall) and CSC 223 (Spring). Aligns with CS Associates.

Prerequisites: DE Computer Science II, DE Pre-Calculus, coreq DE Calculus

Pathway Connections: Earn NOVA AS in CS

DE Cybersecurity I

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: This course focuses on the evolving and pervasive technological environment with an emphasis on securing personal, organizational, and national information. Students will be introduced to the principles of cybersecurity, explore emerging technologies, examine threats and protective measures, and investigate the diverse high-skill, high-wage, and high-demand career opportunities in the field of cybersecurity.

Designation Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA classes are ITN 106 AND ITN 107 (Fall) and ITE 152 AND ITN 101 (Spring). Algins with Cybersecurity Associates.

Prerequisites: Strongly recommend DE Web Page Design and Multimedia.

Pathway Connections: DE Cybersecurity II

DE Cybersecurity II

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Computer Network Software covers computer support and network administration topics. Students learn networking concepts, from usage to components, and create peer-to-peer network systems and client server networks.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA courses are ITN 260 AND ITN 261 (Fall) and ITN 262 AND ITN 263 (Spring). Aligns with Cybersecurity Associates.

Prerequisites: DE Cybersecurity I.

Pathway connections: DE Cybersecurity III

DE Cybersecurity III

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Computer Network Software Operations, Advanced covers advanced aspects of network administration, focusing on the management and support of network users and systems. Topics include understanding the responsibilities of computer professionals, training end users, evaluating new technology, developing system policies, troubleshooting workstations, managing network services and protocols, and effectively using email and business communications.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA courses are ITN 200 AND ITN 266 (Fall) and ITN 260 (Spring). Aligns with Cybersecurity Associates.

Prerequisites: DE Cybersecurity II.

Pathway connections: Earns NOVA AS in Cybersecurity

DE Database Design & Management

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: This course includes database design and Structured Query Language (SQL) programming. Students study database fundamentals, including database development, modeling, design, and normalization.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA classes are ITE 140 (Fall) and ITD 256 (Spring). Aligns with NOVA Web Certificate.

Prerequisites: Strongly recommend DE Web Page Design and DE computer programming.

Pathway connections: NOVA Web Certficate

Graphics Communication Systems

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Graphic Communications System focuses on creating computer graphic designs for display on the World Wide Web. Students acquire knowledge regarding the difference between Web graphics and print graphics.

Prerequisites: None.

Pathway connections: None.

Digital Animation

Where: Offered at ACC

Overview: Students gain experiences related to computer animation by using graphics and design concepts. Students solve problems involving 2D and 3D object manipulation, storyboarding, texturing/mapping, lighting concepts, and environmental geometry.

Prerequisites: None.

Pathway connections: None.

Game Design

Category: Offered at ACHS, Langston, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown.

Overview: Introduction to Game Design in Unity. A good introductory class for students who have no programming experience.

Prerequisites: None

Pathway connections: Programming

Robotics

Category: Offered at ACHS, Langston, Wakefield, W-L, and Yorktown

Overview: Students engage in the study of automation systems, microprocessors, and their applications in manufacturing, transportation, and communication systems. Topics include computer equipment and operating systems, robotics, programming, control systems, and social/cultural impact of these technologies.

Prerequisites: None

Pathway connections: Programming

IB Computer Science SL/HL I

Where: Offered at W-L

Overview: Year one of a two year course for programming in Java and Python. No IB Exam in Year 1, but possible certification in Python.

Designation: International Baccalaureate

Prerequisites: Completion of Algebra II, highly recommended completion of Programming

Pathway connections: IB CS HL II, AP CS A

DE Web Page Design and Multimedia.

Where Offered at ACC

Overview: Students develop proficiency in designing and creating desktop-published projects, multimedia presentations/projects, and Web sites using industry-standard application software. Students apply principles of layout and design in completing projects.

Designation: Dual Enrolled at NOVA. NOVA courses are ITD 110 (Fall) and ITD 210 (Spring). Aligns with NOVA Web Certificate

Prerequisites: None.

Pathway connections: DE Computer Programming I, DE Cybersecurity I, Graphics Communication Systems, Digital Animation

IB Computer Science HL II

Overview: Second course to IB CS HL I. Internal Assessment project completed during the year, and IB Exam in May.

Designation: International Baccalaureate

Prerequisites: IB CS SL/HL I

Pathway connections: None
