AP Computer Science Principles

Category: NOVA-aligned, Home school friendly

Overview: Introduces the big ideas of computing, including data, the internet, algorithms, and the impact of computing on society.

Prerequisites: Algebra I recommended; comfort with problem solving.

Typical grade levels: 9–12

Pathway connections: Great first AP course; prepares students for AP CS A and NOVA CS/IT pathways.

AP Computer Science A

Category: NOVA-aligned, Home school friendly

Overview: Focuses on object-oriented programming in Java, including classes, arrays, inheritance, and algorithmic problem solving.

Prerequisites: Strong Algebra II skills; prior programming experience or AP CS Principles is helpful.

Typical grade levels: 10–12

Pathway connections: Aligns with introductory CS courses at NOVA and many universities.

Programming

Category: NOVA-aligned, Home school friendly

Overview: Introductory programming course using a high-level language (such as Python or JavaScript) to build problem-solving skills.

Prerequisites: Basic algebra and comfort with computers.

Typical grade levels: 9–11

Pathway connections: Serves as a foundation for AP CS courses and dual-enrollment CS.

Computer Programming Advanced

Category: NOVA-aligned

Overview: Builds on introductory programming with more complex data structures, algorithms, and larger projects.

Prerequisites: Programming or AP CS course, or equivalent experience.

Typical grade levels: 10–12

Pathway connections: Prepares students for dual-enrollment CS and advanced college coursework.

Introduction to Information Technology

Category: NOVA-aligned, Home school friendly

Overview: Survey of IT topics including hardware, software, networking, cybersecurity basics, and digital citizenship.

Prerequisites: None.

Typical grade levels: 9–10

Pathway connections: Gateway course for cybersecurity and IT support pathways.

DE Computer Science I

Category: Dual Enrollment (NOVA-aligned)

Overview: College-level introduction to computer science, covering programming fundamentals and problem solving.

Prerequisites: Strong math background and prior programming recommended.

Pathway connections: May earn college credit toward CS degrees at NOVA.

DE Computer Science II

Category: Dual Enrollment (NOVA-aligned)

Overview: Continues CS I with data structures, algorithms, and more complex software design.

Prerequisites: DE Computer Science I or equivalent.

DE Computer Science III

Category: Dual Enrollment (NOVA-aligned)

Overview: Advanced topics such as algorithm analysis, software engineering practices, or specialized CS areas.

Prerequisites: DE Computer Science II.

DE Cybersecurity I

Category: Dual Enrollment, Cybersecurity

Overview: Introduces cybersecurity concepts, threats, and basic defensive strategies.

Prerequisites: Introduction to IT or equivalent.

DE Cybersecurity II

Category: Dual Enrollment, Cybersecurity

Overview: Expands on Cybersecurity I with hands-on labs, network security, and system hardening.

Prerequisites: DE Cybersecurity I.

DE Cybersecurity III

Category: Dual Enrollment, Cybersecurity

Overview: Advanced cybersecurity topics such as incident response, forensics, or ethical hacking concepts.

Prerequisites: DE Cybersecurity II.

DE Database Design & Management

Category: Dual Enrollment, IT

Overview: Covers relational database concepts, SQL, and data modeling.

Prerequisites: Introductory programming or IT course recommended.

Graphics Communication Systems

Category: Digital Media & Design

Overview: Focuses on visual communication, layout, and digital graphics tools.

Digital Animation

Category: Digital Media & Design

Overview: Introduces animation principles and software for creating motion graphics and short animations.

Game Design

Category: Digital Media & Design, Home school friendly

Overview: Covers game mechanics, storytelling, and basic implementation using a game engine or framework.

Robotics

Category: Engineering & CS

Overview: Combines programming with hardware to design, build, and control robots.

IB Computer Science SL

Category: IB

Overview: Standard Level IB course covering core CS concepts, programming, and an internal assessment project.

IB Computer Science HL I

Category: IB

Overview: First year of Higher Level IB CS, with deeper coverage of algorithms and systems.

IB Computer Science HL II

Category: IB

Overview: Second year of HL CS, focusing on advanced topics and exam preparation.
