Sweet Potato Casserole

This is the classic recipe my family has on Thanksgiving. Every year, this is the recipe we bring to the table. It is absolutely delicious. I even remeber my grandfather saying, "This is the greatest casserole I've ever tasted." It is a family favorite at Thanksgiving.

Tips, Tricks, and Substitutions:

  • Use brown sugar instead of pecans. I know a lot of people like pecans on top, but use brown sugar clumps.
  • Mash your sweet potatoes very well. Act like you're making mashed potatoes. The finer, the better.
  • It's okay if you overbake slightly. Some people like it crunchy, and others don't
  • Personalize it. I'm not your boss, I'm simply here to help.
Ingredients: 5 sweet potatoes, 4 cups brown sugar, 1 stick butter, 1 tbsp salt
Step 1: Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit
Step 2: Peel of the skin of the sweet potatoes
Step 3: Mash sweet potatoes in a bowl until there are zero clumps
Step 4: Add salt to the sweet potatoes and mix
Step 5: Spread out the sweet potatoes on a tray, make sure it covers the whole tray
Step 6: Melt the butter
Step 7: In a bowl, add the brown sugar and the butter
Step 8: Mix the two until there is no more butter
Step 9: Make crumbles of brown sugar and plate on top of the sweet potatoes
Step 10: Bake in the oven for about 40 mintes
Step 11: Take out of the oven and enjoy!
This recipe serves 8 and has a total prep time of about 50 minutes
